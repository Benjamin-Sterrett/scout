# Discovery Findings — ChatGPT
**Date:** 2026-04-01
**Entertainment rank:** TBD (pending all 7)

---

**NOTE:** ChatGPT did not answer the discovery questions. Instead, it produced a meta-analysis of the prompt itself — a "research brief" that re-frames the 10 topics into formal research questions, defines methodology, evidence hierarchies, deliverable formats, timelines, roles, acceptance criteria, and a QA checklist. It's a plan for how to do the research, not the research itself.

## What ChatGPT Actually Produced

1. **Extracted Research Questions (RQ1-RQ8)** — Restated the 10 topics as formal research questions
2. **Scope & Boundaries** — Listed platforms, work types, out-of-scope items
3. **Operating Assumptions to Confirm** — Called out open variables (ROI model thresholds, compliance posture, delivery definition, identity strategy)
4. **Evidence Hierarchy** — Tier 1 (official docs) → Tier 2 (direct observation) → Tier 3 (industry analyses) → Tier 4 (anecdotal)
5. **7 Deliverables (A-G)** — Platform matrix, category matrix, search playbook, pricing brief, risk register, execution recommendations, competitive snapshot
6. **Output Formats** — Markdown report + CSV tables + scoring inputs appendix
7. **14-Day Timeline with Gantt** — Day-by-day milestones
8. **Roles & Skills** — Technical researcher, policy analyst, data analyst, optional legal reviewer
9. **Success Criteria & Acceptance Checklist** — Pass/fail conditions for each deliverable
10. **Methodology Trade-offs Table** — When to use desk research vs sampling vs experiments vs interviews

## Unique Contributions (actually useful for Scout)

- **Open variables identified** — ROI model definition, compliance posture, delivery definition, identity strategy, initial category bounds. These are real gaps we need to close before building.
- **Evidence hierarchy** — Tier 1-4 ranking system is useful for weighing conflicting findings across the other 6 LLMs.
- **"Assumptions to confirm" framing** — The scoring engine needs numeric thresholds and explicit definitions, not qualitative guidance. Good reminder.
- **Acceptance checklist** — Can reuse this to evaluate the other 6 LLMs' outputs.
- **Methodology options table** — Policy-first → structured sampling → controlled experiments → expert interviews. Smart sequencing.
- **CSV deliverable spec** — platform_matrix.csv, category_matrix.csv, pricing_benchmarks.csv, risk_register.csv. Worth building these as actual artifacts during synthesis.

## What It Missed

- Zero actual findings about any platform
- No pricing data
- No competitive landscape data
- No legal/compliance specifics
- Didn't answer a single one of its own research questions
- The 14-day timeline assumes a human research team, not an LLM session

## Bottom Line

ChatGPT treated the prompt as a project brief to be analyzed, not a research task to be executed. The meta-analysis is genuinely useful as a framework for synthesizing the other 6 responses — but it produced zero primary research.
